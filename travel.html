Getting to San Francisco

There are two major airports close to San Francisco:

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO)
  • Oakland International Airport (OAK)

Both are served by the BART, as well as have Cab/Uber/Lyft pick up areas. Depending on your arrival time, the BART may be the fastest way to go, especially if flying into Oakland as rush hour traffic across the Oakland Bay Bridge can cause significant delays.

San Francisco Airport

Getting to the BART system

From the San Francisco Airport, the BART station is located at the Garage G stop on the AirTrain -- an autonomous train going between terminals. Once you leave the security area, look for the signs for the AirTrain -- you will have to go up the escalator from arrivals and across the bridge toward the parking garage. The train is free and runs every 4 minutes or so. You can take whichever train arrives first (Red or Blue), as both stop at the BART station (the Blue train is a little faster, especially if you are at Terminal 3).

Once you disembark at Garage G/BART, there is an automated ticket vending machine. It will issue a prepaid ticket. If you're going to downtown San Francisco -- Powell St, Montgomery St, or Embarcadero stops -- the fare is $8.95. They have a fare calculator to look it up ahead of time, as well.

Make sure to keep your BART ticket (even if you paid the exact amount), as you must insert the ticket to exit the system and it will deduct the fare on exit.

Taxi/Lyft/Uber

If you are going to use cabs in San Francisco, I find the Flywheel app to be convenient for finding a cab. The far from the airport to downtown should be somewhere between $50 and $60. There are not enough taxis in San Francisco and flagging one on the street can be difficult. There should be a number of taxis at the airport, however.

Uber and Lyft are also allowed to pick up at the airport, at the departure level (conveniently, that's where you find yourself on exit). Their fares fluctuate on demand, but are usually cheaper than a cab.

Oakland Airport

Getting here

Take BART to the Powell Street Station, which is six blocks south of the Club. From there you can take the Powell Street Cable Car line straight up (north) the six blocks north, or walk up the hill to California Street (this would be quite a workout!).

The Powell Street and California Street cable car lines intersect at our corner. The Powell Street line starts downtown below Union Square and runs all the way to Fisherman's Wharf, while the California Street line runs from the Financial District cross town to Van Ness Avenue.

The California Street line (#1) runs east along Sacramento Street and west along Clay Street which are parallel streets one block apart. Get off at the stop at Sacramento and Powell or the stop at Clay and Powell, depending upon which direction you are traveling.
